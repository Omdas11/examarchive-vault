## Syllabus

| unit_number | syllabus_content | lectures | tags |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Morphology: Modified roots, stems and leaves, phyllotaxy, inflorescence types, aestivation, placentation, flower as modified shoot, fruits, seeds and role of morphology in taxonomy. |  | morphology,inflorescence,fruits |
| 2 | Introduction to Plant Taxonomy: Definition and components of taxonomy, herbarium, botanical gardens, flora, taxonomic keys, evidences from morphology, palynology, cytology, phytochemistry and molecular data, and taxonomic hierarchy. |  | taxonomy,herbarium,flora |
| 3 | Botanical Nomenclature: Binomial nomenclature, ICN, principles and rules, ranks and names, typification, author citation, effective and valid publication, priority and naming of hybrids. |  | nomenclature,icn,typification |
| 4 | Classification: Artificial, natural and phylogenetic classification, Bentham and Hooker, Engler and Prantl, Takhtajan, numerical taxonomy and cladistics. |  | classification,cladistics,systematics |
| 5 | Angiosperm Families: Distinguishing characters and economic importance of Magnoliaceae, Asteraceae, Solanaceae, Lamiaceae, Euphorbiaceae, Moraceae, Orchidaceae, Liliaceae, Musaceae and Poaceae, with orchid floral morphology. |  | families,orchidaceae,poaceae |
